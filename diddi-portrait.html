Dieter "Didi" Hallervorden

This portrait shows Mr. Hallervorden. When I was a child, he was one of the few comedians we were allowed to see, which was funny. When I found a newspaper article with his portrait, I intuitively grabbed it and took it to my palette.

It took three sessions to complete. From the first sketch und underdrawing, to a more evolved block in with more in-depth values to the final finish with details, like hair and pupils. In between, weeks have passed for the paint to dry.

  • Created: 2014
  • Materials: Oil on canvas
  • Styles: Modern & Figurative

About

Stephan is a classical realist artist from Berlin. He specializes in oil portrait, but is also skilled in still life and landscape painting and likes to slip into the pastel medium.

Now and then, he makes a sculpture. He has done both private and corporate commissions. As his second vocation as a psychotherapist suggests, he enjoys working with people.

Stephan's style is a combination of classical and direct painting methods. His paintings express gratitude, joy and an exploration of the issues one faces at different stages of life.

As a new father, he has found his voice painting portraits and images of flowers that intimately convey the joys and struggles in his life.
